## Internal Audit Findings (Claude's analysis)

### Strong elements — keep these
- **Live Convex stats with count-up animation** — single strongest element on the page. Real numbers updating live is a genuine differentiator.
- **"I'm my own first customer" trust nudge** — honest, disarming, right for this ICP.
- **4-week timeline** — gives structure, reduces anxiety.
- **Agent Pokemon card concept** — memorable, scannable, makes agents feel tangible.
- **"Not a demo. That's real output from this week."** — strong copy.

### Problems identified (priority order)

| # | Issue | Detail |
|---|-------|--------|
| 1 | **Hero CTA doesn't convert** | Primary CTA is "See how it works" (scrolls down). Secondary is "Watch them work live" (leaves the page). A ready-to-buy visitor has NO path to act from the fold. |
| 2 | **Headline frames wrong promise** | "Your SEO team that never clocks out" speaks to a tactic. The ICP wants operational leverage — to stop being the bottleneck. Should frame the transformation, not the mechanism. |
| 3 | **"Real Work" section risks audience mismatch** | The questions/briefs shown are from AI/SaaS/startups Reddit — thelaunch.space's own niche. The ICP (healthcare, legal, finance, education) may look at this and think "this isn't for my world." |
| 4 | **"DIY Kickstart" naming is self-defeating** | The ICP is explicitly burned out on DIY. Calling the entry tier "DIY" on a done-for-you page creates doubt at conversion. |
| 5 | **Krishna is invisible** | The ICP "hires people, not products. Trust > Features." Krishna doesn't appear anywhere — no photo, no one-line credibility anchor. Page reads like a product page, not a people page. |
| 6 | **Zero social proof or outcome data** | Operational proof exists (live agent output) but no outcome proof (traffic, rankings, citations). Skeptical ICP needs to see what the activity *produces*. |
| 7 | **Floating CTA competes with itself** | "Watch live" and "Get my AI team" side-by-side in the persistent bar. Half of floating CTA clicks leave the page to /launch-control. |
| 8 | **No objection handling** | ICP's hidden fears (from ICP doc) — "What if it doesn't work?", "What if I'm not technical enough?", "What if I'm dependent forever?" — are never addressed on the page. |
| 9 | **Section-to-section flow has no connective tissue** | Sections feel like independent modules, not a story. No bridge lines pulling the reader forward. |

## Krishna's Constraints (Established This Session)

- **No refunds / risk reversal** — only wants serious buyers. High-touch, selective. Right call.
- **No testimonials yet** — 12-day-old product. Can't manufacture social proof.
- **Perplexity/Reddo citations exist** — Vidura tracks this. Picks a keyword → sends to Perplexity → checks if thelaunch.space blogs appear in citations. This IS outcome proof. "Our content gets cited in AI answers" is a powerful ICP-relevant signal.
- **SEO is the only use case for now** — don't hint at other use cases yet. Avoid confusion. Future expansion is planned but not on this page.
- **Flow should be smooth** — sections should pull the reader forward with a clear reason to keep reading.
- **Sections should showcase outcomes and values, not feature descriptions.**

## Key Insights Arrived At

### 1. Perplexity citations = the outcome proof this page needs right now
Without testimonials or case studies, this is the best available "result." Framing: *"Our content shows up in AI search answers"* — for a 35-50 domain expert who's watching AI eat search, this lands harder than a blog count. It's verifiable, it's future-oriented, and it speaks to the ICP's fear of being left behind.

### 2. The headline frame should shift from tactic → transformation
**Current:** "Your SEO team that never clocks out" (tactic)
**Better direction:** "Your expertise, showing up where your clients are searching. Automatically." (transformation)
This speaks to the ICP's actual desire: to stop being invisible online without having to manage a content function.

### 3. Proposed narrative arc (not yet implemented)
```
Section 1 — Hook: Name the transformation (not the tactic)
    ↓ "How does it actually work?"
Section 2 — The mechanism: What happens every day
    ↓ "Okay but is this real? Show me proof"
Section 3 — Proof of outcomes: Citations + live work + real numbers
    ↓ "Who's doing this work?"
Section 4 — Meet the team: Agents with context (earned now)
    ↓ "What does working together actually look like?"
Section 5 — The 4-week timeline
    ↓ "What does it cost?"
Section 6 — Pricing: Anchored in established value
    ↓ "I'm in — how do I start?"
Section 7 — Lead capture
```

**Key structural change:** Proof comes *before* team cards. Currently the ICP meets the agents before they believe the system works. Flip it — earn trust first, then introduce who does the work.
